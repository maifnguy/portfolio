Kyoto My Hometown

Introducing my favorite spots in Kyoto, Japan!

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

When in Kyoto, definitely visit the bamboo grove in Arashiyama! When the bamboos sway in the wind, it is almost ethereal. Arashiyama is also known for their bamboo products. There are many specialty shops in the area where you can find handcrafted items, such as bamboo baskets.

Fushimi Inari Shrine

One of Kyoto's major tourist attractions, Fushimi Inari Shrine is known for the thousands of red torii gates that snake up Mount Inari. There are also many statues of foxes, that are thought to be the god's messengers. The seemingly endless torii gates appear in the film, "Memoirs of a Geisha".

Kiyomizu-Dera Temple

Surrounded by cherry blossoms in the spring, and red leaves in the fall, Kiyomizu-Dera Temple embraces the seasonal colors of Japan. One of UNESCO's world heritage sites, it is known for the wooden stage that has a great view of the colorful leaves, and the city of Kyoto.

Nijo Castle

Nijo Castle is beautifully preserved, and conveniently located a short bus ride away from Kyoto Station. The wooden floors were designed to creak with each step to ensure that no one could pass through the corridors undetected. The entrance gate is also exquisite, adorned with wood carvings and gold plates.

Nanzenji Temple

One of the most important Zen temples in Japan, Nanzenji Temple is located in the Higashiyama Mountains of Kyoto. Surrounded by trees, the temple is extremely peaceful and relaxing. Outside the main hall is a brick waterway, which was used to transport goods between Kyoto and its neighboring prefecture.

Nishiki Market

Nishiki Market is the best place to go when you're hungry, and want to try a variety of different foods. From fresh seafood to Japanese mochi snacks, Nishiki has bite-size foods that will satisfy your appetite. There are also many cute souvenir shops, and an excellent selection of high-quality Japanese cooking knives.
